Carbon Current

Product Design, UX/UI Design, Internet of Things

 

Overview

Carbon Current is an IoT integrated Mobile App that provides carbon quantification through energy monitoring and optimization for a home environment. It aims to make users mindful of their energy consumption and ecological footprint by incentivizing a low carbon lifestyle in a playful manner.

This is an graduate thesis project with the research concentration on interaction design with IoT and sustainable design. The mobile app concentrates on achieving a stellar UI and seamless user experience.
 

Duration - Ongoing  |  Role - Design Lead and Developer
Software - Sketch, Invision, Adobe Creative Suite

 
 
 
 

Ideation

Carbon Current is an IoT integrated mobile application that incentivizes a low carbon lifestyle through energy monitoring and carbon quantification for the home environment. It aims to make users mindful of their energy consumption and carbon footprint by identifying opportunities for saving. It allows people to donate the energy saved to help people and communities that do not have access to vital resources like energy through third-party, non-profit organizations like Kiva and Gridmates..

The energy is monitored using an IoT device that sends data to the mobile app. The user can pair the app with existing energy monitors like Nest, Sense etc or can get the Carbon Current DIY-kit that can be easily assembled at home. Users can visualize their consumption patterns and translate it into a carbon equivalent. The app provides insights on how the user's consumption is, in comparison to different entities, thus establishing a perspective. The energy reduced monthly is translated in terms of money saved and can be donated to help sustainable causes around the world.

Carbon Current aims to address the critical global issue of energy disparity and climate change and offers individuals an agency to make a difference. It speculates how emerging smart technologies can influence behavioral patterns and eventually lead to a social impact.

 

Goals

  1. Optimize home energy by showing consumption patterns and opportunities to save.
  2. Establish scale and perspective through visualization and comparison, instead of numeric data.
  3. Build a community of users that share the same goals and incentives.
  4. Make users aware and mindful of their carbon emissions
  5. Donate the energy saved to people and communities.
     
 

Research

How it works

 
 

Wireframes

The first couple of wireframes were built in order to consolidate the concept and build a proof of concept prototype. They tested the concept, user flow and long term engagement.


1. The first wireframes were created to test incentives for saving money and carbon reduction, evaluated the feedback on rewards as opposed to penalties.

 

2. The second set of wireframes were created after the concept was better developed. They mainly focused on user flow - discovery and onboarding through a web interface and carbon tracking through a mobile interface. It helped in better understanding where information needed to be abstracted and what would be the major goals of the users.

 

Hardware Testing

The mobile interface gets it's data from a physical IoT device that can be attached to the main electrical board of the home. It would monitor energy by keeping track of the duration, voltage, current and times of energy consumption.

This prototype was created to test the working of a current sensor and power switching. Here, the user can remote turn a lamp on and off from a web interface through a Raspberry Pi.

 

Previous Prototype

The latest prototype is in the form of low-fi mobile app screens. The goal was to establish a visual design language - color scheme, visual elements etc. and perform a functional user test.

 

User Testing (Scenario Based Testing)

For the purpose of user testing, I had to first identify the main assumptions that needed to be tested/validated. I was evaluating how the user felt at different points while I narrated the scenario. Testing how satisfied they were, what frustrated them, and at the same time, asking them to speak out what was confusing.

Objectives :

To understand how users respond to energy patterns

To understand what information needed to be conveyed through the visualization

To understand what incentives would provide motivation

Goals :

Test energy pattern visualization to find out if it can incentivize users to save energy.

Test reward based incentives to find out about user preferences (monetary / verbal).

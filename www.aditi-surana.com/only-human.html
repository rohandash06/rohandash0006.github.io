Only Human

Web Design, UI Design, Front-end Development

 

Overview

Only Human is a Web and iPad app that informs people about the positive and negative media associated with famous figures who have historically been labeled in a binary way. It aims to give users the option of forming their own opinion about the figure through unbiased media.

The project was the winner of Judge's choice/Hot Button category during the A&E History Design Jam conducted by A&E networks in collaboration with Parsons School of Design and NYC Media Lab.


Duration - 2 days  |  Role - Design  |  Team partners - Shikha Singh, Raha Ghassemi

 
 

Challenge 

In November 2015, A+E Networks collaborated with Parsons School of Design and NYC Media Lab to facilitate the History IRL Design Jam. The jam was an opportunity for the Design and Technology students to rethink their relationship with history. The students were challenged to brainstorm, ideate, design, prototype and present their design pitch to the members of the creative, product, and digital media teams at A+E Networks, all within the span of a weekend.

Students were asked to address one of the following two themes - Cultural Preservation, asking “How do we preserve our connection to history in the digital age?” and “Now vs. Then”, addressing “In what new ways can we visualize and imagine the past?”

 

Ideation

Our team, the Shehzadis, chose the second theme - "Now vs Then". Given the exponential flow of newer technologies and the increasing obsolescence of older ones, we wanted to explore new ways of imaging the past. Being from diverse backgrounds, we challenged the above question from different political, cultural, technological and philosophical standpoints. 

Often times, historical figures are seen as purely good or evil, thus oversimplifying them as human beings. They are not static characters in a novel. People see them in different ways depending on different contexts, such as time period and location. We identified a crucial need to perceive the past in an unbiased manner - something that is constantly lacking from the current media served to us. We saw this as a great opportunity for the juxtaposition of the positive and negative media related to various topics, figures and events in the past.

 
 

Prototype

We designed and coded a web and iPad app to prototype the above idea. In the prototype, as the user slides across the face of the historical figure, he/she starts to discover the positive and negative media about the person. Based on the media, they can formulate an opinion and vote for the figure

This is what we were able to code in a day.

Totem

Web, UI, Front-end Development

 

Overview

Totem is a digital archive of modern, ordinary items or entities and the ritualized practices associated with them in the contemporary age of virtuality and digitization. It stems from a deeper speculation of the effect of emerging technologies on traditional norms of cultural anthropology.
 

Duration - 4 weeks  |  Role - Design Lead and Developer

 

Ideation 

An intriguing aspect of Cultural Anthropology is the study of religion, rituals and mythicism. Rituals and totems have, forever, been a fascinating part of human life and their social existence. They are an innate part of human existence, transcending through various times, cultures, religions and languages. A peculiar attribute of the human psyche is the fetishism of inanimate objects and the symbolic meaning ascribed to them which shapes various social practices and formulates various belief systems. The female figurines symbolized
fertility; the traditional Chinese tea ceremony holds a deeper significance than merely drinking tea; Hindus worship a black stone as it is believed to be the personification of a deity, Shiva. 

Objects have never been mere objects but ideas, signs, feelings and aspirations. As Julia Beck writes in her article, For the Love of Stuff, “Things are repositories for the meaning people project on them.” This fetishism and attribution of significance to inanimate objects has cradled religion and mysticism through centuries. Even today, as we transition from believing in a deity to worshipping the omniscient algorithms, we carry with us the same fetish towards objects, physical or abstract, existent or non-existent.

 

In a digital age, what role does technology play in shaping modern ritualistic practices and what are the objects, physical or virtual, associated with these rituals?

 

The project is a creative exploration of questions like -

  • Can digital objects be ritualistic?
  • What is the symbolic meaning associated with modern objects?
  • How have these objects become so central that their usage has become almost ritualistic?
  • How important is the physicality of the objects involved in a ritual?
     
 

Concept

Totem is a digital archive that holds a collection of objects, both physical and virtual, that hold a deep significance in a person's life in the contemporary digital context. The collection lives in the forms of anecdotes, pictures from people and narrates how and why the entity has become ritualistic in their life. The archive itself posses some mystical characteristics in the way it lives, thus personifying it into being a romantic object rather than the stereotypical data container.

The archive derives its inspiration from ancient ritualistic objects and totems and their significance. It ponders on the intricate relationship individuals have with inanimate objects. It juxtaposes ancient totems of spiritual significance with the modern day objects and narrates the rituals associated to them.

Process

 

1. Collection of Data

The process for this project began by one-on-one interactions with people in order to collect the relevant data to form the archive. This qualitative research methodology helped in understanding people's diverse backgrounds, day-to-day life and emotional attachment to certain objects. It also helped in evaluating the level of technological media present in their lives and it's effect.

The objects were then mapped to the anecdotes and pictures collected to form the essence of the archive.

2. Life of the Archive

The next step was to find a way of ritualistic method of accessing the collection and the archive. It was important to make the archival space resonate with the content it housed. The archive, thus, tries to play on an underlying mystical theme, lets one find their way, and guides them through subtle responses.

“The archive is first the law of what can be said, the system that governs the appearance of statements as unique events. But the archive is also that which determines that all these things said do not accumulate endlessly in an amorphous mass,…nor do they disappear at the mercy of chance external accidents; but they are grouped together in distinct figures…which determines that they do not withdraw at the same pace in time, but shine, as it were, like stars…” - Michel Foucault

3. Visual Design

The final step was to create a visual language for the archive that could represent the underlying theme. For this purpose, I studied ancient totem poles, their meaning and the way they were created. I created a totem pole from the objects collected during research. The logo of the archive itself represented the entire collection of the archive.

 
 

The collection is such that when the user hovers over the items, the modern totems reveal themselves. On clicking a particular item, it's narrative can be viewed. The archive page narrates the story of the archive - it's conception, population and design.

 
 

Demo

The website is coded in HTML, CSS and Javascript. It uses a few libraries to make the interface more organic and mystical.

 
 
 
 

Usability Testing

In order to figure out the usability issues with the web interface, I performed a Heuristic Evaluation with three users. The goal was to compare the web interface against Neilson's Usability Heuristics, mainly "Visibility of system status" and "Aesthetic and minimalist design".

 The goal was to identify the pain points as the user traveled through the interface and fix them according to the feedback.

 
 

Feedback

  1. The terms "collection" and "archive" were ambiguous. The user didn't understand the difference.
  2. The user was not able to figure out that the items were clickable and needed instructions.
  3. The visual design was pleasing.
